Why is Consistency Important?
It develops discipline and self-control

Forming a new habit can be challenging because it’s easier to go back to what you’re comfortable with.

Second Reason
It builds a higher level of trust.

The level of trust you have with yourself shows up in the level of trust others have with you. Being consistent will help build momentum. This means you’ll also feel good about what you’re working towards.

Third Reason
It will create accountability

Your results will reveal if you’re consistent or not. If you know you’re not on track, you can hold yourself accountable. This means you can adjust your actions or behaviours and keep progressing towards what you want.
